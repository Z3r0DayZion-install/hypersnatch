# HyperSnatch - Pre-Order Validation Plan
## Test Economic Commitment Before Building

---

## 1️⃣ CHANGE THE QUESTION

### From Interest to Buying Intent

**Stop Asking:**
- "Is this painful?"
- "Would you try it?"
- "Is this cool?"

**Start Asking:**
- "If this solved that problem cleanly and worked offline, would you pay $149 for it?"
- "If I build this and it does X reliably, would you be willing to pre-order at $99?"

### Why This Matters

**Words are cheap.**
- Curiosity costs nothing
- "Sounds useful" costs nothing
- "Keep me posted" costs nothing

**Pre-orders are signal.**
- Money demonstrates commitment
- Refundable risk shows confidence
- Economic intent validates market

---

## 2️⃣ SIMPLE LANDING PAGE (1 DAY MAX)

### Headline
**Offline HTML/HAR Artifact Reconstruction Tool for Digital Forensic Work**

### Bullet Points
- Parse saved HTML & HAR files offline
- Extract and document accessible endpoints
- Generate structured evidence logs
- No cloud dependency
- Clean export for case documentation

### Call to Action
**Pre-order Beta — $99 (Refundable)**

### Additional Elements
- **What you get:** Beta access + 1 year updates
- **When:** Delivered in 90 days or full refund
- **Why pre-order:** Fund development, get early access
- **Refund policy:** Full refund if not delivered in 90 days

- No ecosystem language

- No technical jargon
- No future roadmap

- **Payment:** Stripe or PayPal
- **Time to build:** 4-6 hours maximum
- **Cost:** $50-100 for domain/hosting

## 3️⃣ PRE-ORDER STRATEGY

### Why Pre-Order?

**Reality Testing:**
- Separates curiosity from commitment
- Forces clarity on value proposition
- Tests price sensitivity
- Validates economic demand

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Refundable reduces customer risk
- Funds development without debt
- Builds early customer base
- Provides validation signal

### Pre-Order Structure

**Price:** $99 (50% discount from planned $149)
- **Rationale:** Early adopter discount
- **Value:** 90-day delivery guarantee
- **Risk:** Full refund if not delivered

**What They Get:**
- Beta access when ready
- 1 year of updates
- Priority support
- Input on features

**Timeline:**
- Delivered in 90 days or full refund
- Regular progress updates
- Transparent development process

---

## 4️⃣ NUMBERS THAT MATTER

### Success Thresholds

**Minimum Viable Signal:**
- **1 pre-order** → Proceed with 90-day build
- **3 pre-orders** → Strong signal, accelerate
- **5 pre-orders** → Full commitment, expand scope

### Failure Thresholds

**Stop Building If:**
- **0 pre-orders** after 30-50 conversations
- **<3 pre-orders** after 60 days
- **No payment** despite positive feedback

### Conversion Metrics

**Conversation to Pre-Order Rate:**
- **Target:** 5-10% conversion
- **Minimum:** 2% conversion
- **Failure:** <1% conversion

**Time to Decision:**
- **Target:** Immediate decision
- **Acceptable:** 24-48 hours
- **Concern:** More than 1 week

## 5️⃣ THE HARD PART

### Resistance Expectation

**You Will Face:**
- "I need to see it first"
- "Budget's tight right now"
- "We already have tools for that"
- "Let me think about it"
- "Can you show me a demo?"

### Why This Happens

**Pre-orders expose reality:**
- Forces genuine consideration
- Removes ambiguity from interest
- Tests actual value proposition
- Reveals true market need

**Interest lets you dream:**
- "Sounds cool" costs nothing
- "Keep me posted" costs nothing
- "Interesting idea" costs nothing

### How to Handle Resistance

**For "Need to see it first":**
- "I understand. That's why it's refundable. If it doesn't work, you get your money back."
- "The pre-order funds the development. No pre-orders = no tool."

**For "Budget's tight":**
- "That's why it's $99 instead of $149. Early adopter discount."
- "You get 1 year of updates included."

**For "Already have tools":**
- "Do those tools work offline? Do they generate evidence logs?"
- "This is specifically for offline artifact reconstruction."

---

## 6️⃣ WORST-CASE OUTCOME

### What You Might Discover

**They Script It Themselves:**
- "We have custom scripts that do this."
- "Our team built something similar."
- **Result:** Market doesn't need commercial solution

**They Use Existing Suites:**
- "We use EnCase/FTK for this."
- "Our firm provides enterprise tools."
- **Result:** Market already served

**Not Painful Enough:**
- "It's not that time-consuming."
- "Manual process works fine."
- **Result:** No compelling need

**Price Too High:**
- "$99 is too much for this."
- "I'd pay $29, not $99."
- **Result:** Price sensitivity discovered

**Offline Not Compelling:**
- "We work online mostly."
- "Cloud tools work fine for us."
- **Result:** Offline assumption wrong

### Why This Is a Win

**Saves 90 Days:**
- No wasted development time
- No opportunity cost
- Clear market understanding
- Better pivot direction

**Provides Clarity:**
- Real market feedback
- Actual price sensitivity
- Genuine need assessment
- Better positioning

---

## 7️⃣ SOFT NO'S VS REAL YES

### Common Soft No's

**"We'd need to see it first":**
- Translation: Not convinced enough to risk money
- Response: "That's why it's refundable. Risk is on me."

**"Hard to justify without demo":**
- Translation: Need more proof than words
- Response: "Demo requires funding. Pre-order funds development."

**"Budget's tight":**
- Translation: Not valuable enough at this price
- Response: "That's why it's discounted. Early adopter pricing."

**"We already use X":**
- Translation: Current solution is sufficient
- Response: "Does X work offline? Generate evidence logs?"

### Real Yes Indicators

**Immediate Payment:**
- No hesitation
- No conditions
- Clear commitment

**Questions About Features:**
- "Will it do X?"
- "Can it export Y?"
- "When will it be ready?"

**Referral Interest:**
- "My colleague might want this"
- "Who else is pre-ordering?"

---

## 8️⃣ RED FLAGS TO WATCH

### "Cool But No Pay"

**Warning Signs:**
- "This is really cool, but..."
- "Interesting concept, but..."
- "Love the idea, but..."

**What This Means:**
- Hobby product, not business
- Technical interest, not economic need
- Nice to have, not must have

### Response to Red Flags

**Don't:**
- Build anyway hoping they'll pay later
- Assume they'll pay when it's ready
- Think "interest" will convert to "payment"

**Do:**
- Acknowledge the feedback
- Ask what would make it worth paying for
- Consider pivoting to different market

---

## 9️⃣ DECISION TREE

### After 30-50 Outreach Conversations

**1-5 Pre-Orders: BUILD**
- Signal validated
- Market confirmed
- Proceed with 90-day build

**0 Pre-Orders: PIVOT**
- No economic demand
- Problem doesn't exist
- Find different market

**Mixed Responses: NARROW**
- Some interest, no payment
- Refine target user
- Test different messaging

### No Exceptions

**This is binary:**
- Payment = proceed
- No payment = stop
- No middle ground

## 🔟 HARD TRUTH

### Builders vs. Founders

**Builders Who Spin for Years:**
- Talk about features
- Focus on architecture
- Collect "interest"
- Build without validation
- Chase funding without revenue

**Founders Who Ship Profitable Tools:**
- Test money early
- Focus on customer problems
- Collect pre-orders
- Build with validation
- Chase revenue, not funding

### The Uncomfortable Part

**You Must:**
- Ask for money before building
- Face rejection directly
- Accept "no" as market feedback
- Pivot if market doesn't exist
- Risk looking foolish for asking

**You Cannot:**
- Hide behind "interest"
- Build hoping they'll pay later
- Assume "cool" equals "valuable"
- Blame market for not understanding
- Justify failure with "timing"
