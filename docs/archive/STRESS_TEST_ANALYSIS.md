## 1️⃣ VALIDATE MARKET DEMAND (WITHOUT ASSUMPTION)

### Who is actively searching for "Link Resurrection" today?

**Answer: No one.**

The term "Link Resurrection" does not exist in market searches. Companies are searching for:
- "Web scraping tools" (but these are blocked by compliance)
- "Digital forensics software" (but these are heavy, expensive suites)
- "Link checker tools" (but these are simple validation utilities)
- "Archive access tools" (but these are for web archives, not local files)

### What budget line item would this come out of?

**Unclear mapping.**

Potential budget sources:
- **IT Operations**: Unlikely - they use existing tools or build custom solutions
- **Legal/Compliance**: Possible - but they have established vendors (Relativity, DISCO)
- **Security Operations**: Unlikely - they use SIEMs and threat intel platforms
- **Digital Preservation**: Small market - libraries, archives, academic institutions

**Problem**: No clear budget owner with existing line item for this specific capability.

### What existing tool would it replace?

**Direct replacement: Nothing.**

- **Web Scrapers**: Different use case (live vs. static)
- **Forensic Suites**: Much broader scope, more expensive
- **Link Checkers**: Much simpler functionality
- **Archive Tools**: Different domain (web archives vs. local files)

**Reality**: HyperSnatch creates a new category, not replacing existing spend.

### What measurable pain justifies $499–$2,999?

**Weak pain justification.**

- **$499 (Core)**: Individual researchers might pay, but market is small
- **$2,999 (Enterprise)**: Requires clear ROI justification
- **Pain Points**: Link decay, artifact loss, compliance requirements
- **Counterargument**: Organizations accept link loss as cost of doing business

**Conclusion**: Market demand is assumed, not demonstrated. Valuation assumptions collapse without clear demand signal.
